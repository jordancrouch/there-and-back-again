Inveruglass II

Travel

For our second journey back to Inveruglass, we drove the 1000+ mile round-trip from South Wales, with an overnight stay at Lockerbie on the way up. Driving allowed for less restrictions with our luggage compared to flying, and the roads north of the Scottish Border make for an enjoyable drive. This also meant that we could take a slight detour to Falkirk to catch a glimpse of the Kelpies, as well as visit a magical Harry Potter themed shop, The Lonely Broomstick.

Kintulloch

This lovely bungalow near Kingussie, south of Inverness, is a great location for any nature lover. Surrounded by beautiful landscapes, falls, forest walks, and wildlife, there is no shortage of options to explore.

Pattack Falls

In slightly dreary weather, we put our waterproofs on and took a walk around Pattack Falls in Newtonmore, located about 35 minutes drive away.

Highland Wildlife Park

Only a 20 minute drive away, we took another visit back to the Highland Wildlife Park. The safari park contains Red Deer, Elk, Bison, Przewalski's wild horses, Vicuna, and Bukhara deer. The park itself homes a range of different animals, including: Amur tigers, Arctic foxes, European grey wolves, Northern lynx, Snow leopards, Wildcats, Polar bears, and Wolverine, to name a few. We visited daily talks for the Snow leapords, Polar bears, Amur tigers, Wildcats, and European grey wolves.

Northern Lights

The evening after our trip to the Highland Wildlife Park, there was strong electromagnetic activity, and we were incredibly lucky to see the Northern Lights for the first time! Unfortunately the cloud cover was quite dense, but it was still amazing to see.

Blair Castle & Gardens

About an hour drive away, Blair Castle and Gardens is steeped in history. The castle has been home to the Atholl family for over seven centuries, and it's history starts as early as 1260, with the gardens containing some of the UK's tallest trees.

Falls of Truim

Less than 30 minutes drive away is the Falls of Truim and Truim woods.
