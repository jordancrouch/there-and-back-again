Inveruglass II

Travel

For our second journey back to Inveruglass, we drove the 1000+ mile round-trip from South Wales, with an overnight stay at Lockerbie on the way up. Driving allowed for less restrictions with our luggage compared to flying, and the roads north of the Scottish Border make for an enjoyable drive. This also meant that we could take a slight detour to Falkirk to catch a glimpse of the Kelpies, as well as visit a magical Harry Potter themed shop, The Lonely Broomstick.

Kintulloch

This lovely bungalow near Kingussie, south of Inverness, is a great location for any nature lover. Surrounded by beautiful landscapes, falls, forest walks, and wildlife, there is no shortage of options to explore.

Pattack Falls

In slightly dreary weather, we put our waterproofs on and took a walk around Pattack Falls in Newtonmore, located about 35 minutes drive away.

Highland Wildlife Park

Only a 20 minute drive away, we took another visit back to the Highland Wildlife Park. The safari park contains Red Deer, Elk, Bison, Przewalski's wild horses, Vicuna, and Bukhara deer. The park itself homes a range of different animals, including: Amur tigers, Arctic foxes, European grey wolves, Northern lynx, Snow leopards, Wildcats, Polar bears, and Wolverine, to name a few. We visited daily talks for the Snow leapords, Polar bears, Amur tigers, Wildcats, and European grey wolves.

Northern Lights

The evening after our trip to the Highland Wildlife Park, there was strong electromagnetic activity, and we were incredibly lucky to see the Northern Lights for the first time! Unfortunately the cloud cover was quite dense, but it was still amazing to see.

Blair Castle & Gardens

About an hour drive away, Blair Castle and Gardens is steeped in history. The castle has been home to the Atholl family for over seven centuries, and it's history starts as early as 1260, with the gardens containing some of the UK's tallest trees.

Falls of Truim

Less than 30 minutes drive away is the Falls of Truim and Truim woods.

Jedburgh

Travel

This was our first time driving, which is over 700 miles round-trip from South Wales. Not only was this much easier with luggage, but it was also very cheap, with being able to drive one-way on one tank of fuel. We stayed at Micawber Cottage in Jedburgh, only a few minutes drive from the town centre.

Jerburgh Abbey, Castle Jail & The Capon Tree

Only minutes away from where we stayed, we visited some of the town's local landmarks, as well as one of the last known surviving trees of the ancient Scottish Jedforest, The Capon Oak Tree.

Kielder Forest & Bird of Prey Centre

Roughly 50 minutes away, what was initially planned to be a walk around Kielder Water and a visit to the Bird of Prey Centre, eventually turned in to spending the majority of the day at the latter. The staff gave two very informative talks, where they would feed different birds and allow audience members the opportunity to wear a gauntlet and have the birds land on their hand.

Edinburgh

We set off early with a 20 minute drive to Tweedbank Railway Station, and a one-hour train journey to Edinburgh. Arriving early afforded us the freedom to explore a quiet Greyfriars Kirkyard to search for some of the headstones that inspired many character names featured in the Harry Potter series, as well as a visit to the Museum Context shop. After some lunch, we visited Edinburgh Castle and The Wallace Monument, before making our return train journey.

Floors Castle

Only half an hour away, Floors Castle is full of fascinating history and both a formal and walled garden.

Inveruglass

Travel

Flying from Bristol Airport to Inverness, which only takes about an hour, we got picked up to drive just over an hour to our home for the week, Kintulloch.

Uath Lochans

Starting the week off strong, we took on the challenge of the Farleitter Crag Trail, and were lucky to have to some great weather for it. Only 15 minutes drive away, this 2.5 mile walk was quite hilly at times, but getting to walk through Scots pine woodland leading to views at the top overlooking the lochans and the Spey valley was more than worth the effort.

Cairngorms Reindeer

After warming up with the walk at Uath Lochans, we faced a bigger challenge with a Hill Trip up into the Cairngorm mountains to see the Reindeer herd. The visitor centre is just over 30 minutes away, but requires an additional 5 minute travel that you will need your own transport for.

Packing for cold weather for this trip is required, and you must also wear suitable walking shoes/boots, as it's unsafe without these items and you won't be allowed on the trip without these essentials.

The 30 minute climb is steep and requires a good level of fitness, but you will be rewarded with being able to get in amongst the herd, whilst learning about their history and reintroduction to the Cairngorms starting in 1952.

Highland Wildlife Park

A 20 minute drive away takes you to the Highland Wildlife Park, which is highly recommended to pay a visit to. Some of the highlights include seeing Brodie, the UK's only polar bear cub, learning about Scottish wildcats and the conservation breeding for release centre they have, and my personal favourite, European grey wolves.

Loch Shiel & Glenfinnan Viaduct

An essential visit for any Harry Potter fans. Loch Shiel is the 4th longest loch in Scotland, and is the inspiration for the Black Lake, where Hogwarts is set upon the banks of. The Glenfinnan Viaduct is probably more recognisable to most, which is used by the Jacobite Steam Train, or the Hogwarts Express in the films.
